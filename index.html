The Problem

Getting denied is easy.
Getting help is the hard part.

01

Denials Have No Next Step

Most families don't understand why their claim was denied — or what they're supposed to do about it. The letter arrives and then nothing. No guidance, no follow-up, no path forward.

02

The Paperwork Never Ends

Getting a claim resolved means collecting documents, visiting offices, and resubmitting — sometimes more than once. Most families give up before it's done. The system rewards persistence and punishes exhaustion.

03

No One Owns the Follow-Up

Community health workers want to help — but they have no standard tool to track a case or file a grievance. Every agent figures it out on their own, every time. Outcomes depend on who you know, not what you do.

How It Works

A clear path
from denied
to covered.

Our process is built around the patient — not the paperwork. We handle the complexity so you can focus on your health.

Start Today
01

Denial Intake

The agent fills out a short form — what happened, where, and what documents the family already has. No training needed. Takes a few minutes.

02

Document Checklist

VerityAid generates a tailored list of exactly what's needed to resolve this specific denial — so agents stop guessing and families stop making wasted trips.

03

Follow-Up Timeline

A clear, step-by-step plan tells the agent what to do and by when — so cases don't fall through the cracks when life gets busy.

04

Grievance Packet

When it's time to escalate, VerityAid generates a ready-to-submit grievance letter and proof-of-process log — everything the family needs, in one place.

05

Shared With the Family

The beneficiary gets a copy. The agent's supervisor gets a copy. Everyone knows what was done, when, and what comes next.

Our Mission

Healthcare is a
right — not a
privilege.

VerityAid was founded on a simple belief: the gap between eligibility and access shouldn't exist. Millions of families in India are covered by PM-JAY — but when a claim is denied, most never see that money. Not because they don't qualify, but because the follow-up is too complex to navigate alone.

We exist to close that gap. Permanently.

  • Radical transparency in every interaction
  • No patient left behind regardless of ability to pay
  • Community-first, technology-enabled advocacy
  • Accountability to outcomes — not activity

"The denial isn't the end. It's just where the real work begins."

— Megha Viswanath, Founder, VerityAid
